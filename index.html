Venue

Naturebridge

Naturebridge is a nonprofit on the shore of Lake Crescent, within the boundary of Olympic National Park. They run hands-on environmental science programs for kids, and host weddings on weekends.

Housing

We're really excited to offer everyone the opportunity to stay on site and spend the entire weekend together. Naturebridge accomodations are a combination of standard and historic cabins, with bunk beds and a combination of private, semi-private and bathhouse restrooms. As part of booking the venue, we were required to book all of their accomodations for the whole weekend. If you stay at Naturebridge with us, you will need to pay for your weekend food and housing after you RSVP. If you choose to sleep elsewhere but still dine with the gang, we would love to have you; you can commit to this option with your RSVP as well.

If you prefer more traditional accomodations, Lake Crescent Lodge is a 10 minute walk/2 minute drive away. They no longer have availability on their website, but if you contact us we know of some rooms that are available.

Regardless of where you stay, we are pleased to be able to offer everyone meals for the whole duration of the weekend for $60 per person.

Travel

Getting to the Olympics

Airport

The best airport to fly into is Seattle-Tacoma (SEA), which is a 2:45 drive from the venue.

Carpooling

There are no good public transit options for getting to the park: a combination of Greyhound and city buses takes over eleven hours. We will help organize carpools for those interested.

Timing

Before buying flights, we encourage you to consider whether you want to spend more time in the area before or after the wedding. There are wonderful backpacking opportunities in the Olympics (which we intend to partake of starting on the morning of July 5). We will be watching fireworks in Port Angeles on the 4th and you're more than welcome to stick around and join us. Please let us know if you're interested!

FAQ

Attendees

Can I bring a guest?

You are welcome to bring a plus-one, but please let us know!

Are kids welcome?

Absolutely! We anticipate 15-20 kids under 12 and a few teenagers. The same way that we hope to schedule fun activities for our adult guests, we welcome ideas for the under-18 crowd. One thing we are considering is running movie nights after dinner. We have a building that we're intending to use mostly for kids, and are happy to help parents organize a rotating kid-watching schedule so that they can enjoy other activities.

Can I bring pets?

NatureBridge does not allow pets in any of the buildings. Dogs can be on leash on the campus and on certain designated trails, but none of the major trails. If the weather is good, we anticipate that much of the activity will be outside, so please talk to us if you'd like to bring a pet and stay elsewhere.

Safety

How are you planning on addressing the pandemic?

For everyone's peace of mind, we ask that all guests over 5 receive covid vaccines at least two weeks before attending our wedding. This is very important to us.

The venue is also requiring that we send everyone a survey shortly before the event with questions about covid symptoms.

What is the weather like in the park in July?

Average highs in early July are in the low 70s, with average lows in the high 40s. However, the weather can be unpredictable. Be sure to bring layers so you will be prepared for all kinds of weather, especially if you intend on hiking. There will be snow at higher elevations on hikes. Sunrise will be around 5:20am and sunset around 9:20pm.

Venue

How do I make hotel reservations?

If you have no changes to your housing needs and are staying with us at NatureBridge, no worries! We'll have your cabin or bunk assignment waiting when you arrive for check in. If you were planning to stay off-site (Lake Crescent, camping at Shadow Mountain, Log Cabin RV & Campground across the lake, Fairholme Campground or in Port Angeles), please make your reservations soon. We hear the 2022 wedding season is filling up fast.

As a reminder about the options on Naturebridge's campus, the seven main cabins (Raccoon, Orchid, Crescent, Osprey, Ouzel, Beardslee, and Chanterelle) are heated and air conditioned, and are clustered around a gazebo and central bath house. They have two, four or six rooms per cabin, each with 6 beds. Three of them also include an in-cabin full bathroom.

There are also six smaller, historic cabins (Honeysuckle, Redwing, Dreamerie, Alabam, Summerie, and Valerie). These are more varied in layout, but all include either a full or half bathroom.

If your housing needs have changed, feel free to submit another housing survey or get in touch with us. We have seen that Lake Crescent Lodge shows no availability Fourth of July weekend on their website. However, if you would like to make reservations there, let us know. We know of a few extra rooms which may be available.

When can I check in and out?

If you are staying at Naturebridge, check in will be at 2:30 pm on Friday July 1st and you will need to check out of your room by 10 am on Monday July 4th. You will be able to stay on campus until 1 pm on the 4th.

Will there be wifi and cell service?

Free wireless internet is available in the main buildings, but it can be intermittent. AT&T wireless devices generally work on campus and Verizon has been occasionally spotty. An emergency landline phone is located in the Fireside Room in Rosemary Inn.

What do I need to bring to stay in the cabins?

Generally, you would need to bring what you would to a stay at a hostel. NatureBridge will provide flat sheet, fitted sheet, pillow, pillowcase, blanket/sleeping bag and a bath towel (various sizes). There is a coin operated washer and dryer in the men's side of the bath house. Guests will need to bring washcloths, soap, shampoo, and hair dryers. If you remember your college days and will be using the bathhouse or a hall bath, you might want to bring flip flops, a robe, and a tote of some sort to carry items to and from the showers.

Do the rooms have closets to hang formal clothes?

Unfortunately not, but most rooms have extra beds that you can lay things out on.

Will we have control over the temperature in our rooms?

The standard cabins have a single central thermostat, so you should plan accordingly if you tend to run hotter or colder than most people. The historic cabins have wall heaters.

What is the plan for other meals during the weekend?

Besides our rehearsal and reception dinners, guests will be provided 3 breakfasts (omelettes, French toast, pancakes), 2 lunches (sandwiches), 1 additional dinner (pizza).

Meals are served buffet style in the dining hall at pre-arranged times

Naturebridge staff will wash dishes, but you will be responsible for bringing your own dishes to the dish tubs near the kitchen.

They can accommodate vegetarian, vegan, gluten and dairy-free special requests.

A refrigerator will be available near the kitchen to store small items of food. There is also an ice machine available for our use.

Can I camp?

Unfortunately, Olympic National Park only allows camping in designated areas. The NatureBridge campus is not a designated camping area and therefore, RVs and tents are not allowed. The closest campgrounds are Fairholme Campground (approximately 6 – 8 miles west of campus on Hwy 101), Shadow Mountain (approximately 6 – 8 miles east of campus on Hwy 101), and Log Cabin Resort campground (approximately 8 miles from campus on East Beach Road.)

Does the National Park impose any other restrictions?

  • No candles indoors
  • No helium balloons
  • No throwing rice or birdseed
  • No fireworks or sparklers
  • Garbage and cigarette butts need to be disposed in a waste container

Activities

What should I do if I think of an awesome activity that I want to run?

Fill out our activity signup and let us know! There's no commitment yet, since we'll reach out as we create a schedule. Of course, you're also welcome to organize impromptu events once the weekend arrives as well.

Will there be activites for kids?

Yes! Whether you're a parent or not, you're welcome to organize activites that kids might be interested in. We're planning on using the classroom building for kids events.

Will there be canoeing?

Yes! Two Naturebridge educators will be running canoe trips in their 17 person canoes both Saturday and Sunday. There will be an opportunity to sign up in June when the schedule of events is posted.

Is there anything I should bring to make the weekend more fun?

Feel free to add items, either those you're planning on bringing to share or those you hope someone else might bring, to our communal spreadsheet. Note that content posted there is publicly viewable, so don't include anything sensitive!
